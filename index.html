BEST: Biomechanics-guided Multimodal Understanding of Human Movement for Sports and Health

A new workshop focused on combining biomechanics, computer vision, multimodal sensing, and machine learning to better understand movement for sports performance, injury prevention, rehabilitation, and everyday health.

VenueBMVC 2026 • Location to be announced
ThemeMultimodal movement intelligence
AudienceResearchers, clinicians, athletes, and practitioners

Why BEST matters

Human movement is rich, dynamic, and increasingly measurable through video, wearable sensors, force plates, and clinical assessment. This workshop brings together experts interested in learning from these complementary modalities to build robust models for sports science and healthcare.

  • Bridging biomechanics and computer vision.
  • Advancing multimodal representation learning for movement.
  • Supporting real-world impact in athletic performance and rehabilitation.

Topics of interest

  • Biomechanics-informed action recognition and pose understanding
  • Multimodal fusion for sports analytics and health monitoring
  • Movement forecasting and injury risk modeling
  • Wearable sensor analysis and clinical motion assessment
  • Foundation models and representation learning for movement data

Workshop goal

Create a collaborative forum for researchers to discuss methods, challenges, and opportunities at the intersection of movement science and machine intelligence.

Who should attend

Computer vision researchers, biomechanists, sports scientists, clinicians, and anyone working on human-centered sensing and behavior modeling.

Expected outcomes

A shared research agenda, new interdisciplinary collaborations, and a stronger community for multimodal movement understanding.
